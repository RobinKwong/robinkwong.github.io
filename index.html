一Welcome

This is a website about the intersection of journalism and technology … plus the most important aspect: people.

I am talking about the people who make their living producing the news at that intersection. Specifically, I am interested in how they organise themselves. I will be using this space to write about how that is changing rapidly. A good place to start would be my post on why newsrooms need project managers.

Who am I? My name is Robin Kwong, and I am Special Projects Editor at the Financial Times. This is my personal website. You can find out more about me below.

五About

I am Special Projects Editor at the Financial Times, where I am bringing project management and design thinking into the newsroom.

I have been a journalist for the last eleven years, most recently as interactive data journalist where I learnt a little bit about how to code and design websites. Before that, I was the tech editor and had also reported from Taiwan and Hong Kong.

I majored in philosophy as an undergraduate at Yale. My dad still wants me to quit my job and get a PhD. (Hi dad!)

I will happily talk about: gardening, games (of both the board and the digital varieties), cooking, and Liverpool FC.

I live with my lovely wife Scarlet and our very spoiled cat, Valentine, in Southeast London.
